Stick Together to Pull Through Difficult Times and Uphold Justiceâ

-- âDirector-General of the Department of Asian Affairs of the Foreign Ministry Liu Jinsong Meets with Pakistani Ambassador to China Moin ul Haque

2022-09-27 23:55

On September 27, 2022, Director-General of the Department of Asian Affairs of the Foreign Ministry Liu Jinsong met with Pakistani Ambassador to China Moin ul Haque.

Liu Jinsong said that China-Pakistan relations are unique and time-tested, and have maintained the good momentum of growth despite the changes in international and regional situations and the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic. Recent days have seen frequent high-level interactions between the two countries. President Xi Jinping met with Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif on the sidelines of the Samarkand Summit of the Shanghai Cooperation Organization. Their meeting steered the course for the development of China-Pakistan relations in the new era and injected strong impetus into the China-Pakistan all-weather friendship. State Councilor and Defense Minister Wei Fenghe met with Chief of Army Staff Qamar Javed Bajwa of Pakistan in Xi'an. State Councilor and Foreign Minister Wang Yi met with Foreign Minister Bilawal Bhutto Zardari on the sidelines of the General Debate of the United Nations General Assembly. China is ready to work with Pakistan to follow through on the important common understanding reached by the leaders of the two countries, promote the development of the all-weather strategic cooperative partnership between the two countries, and accelerate the building of a closer China-Pakistan community with a shared future in the new era.

Liu Jinsong said that Chinese leaders are very concerned about the floods and post-disaster reconstruction in Pakistan. The thoughts of the Chinese government and people are with our âironcladâ friends in the disaster-hit areas of Pakistan. People of all sectors in China are making effort to support Pakistan's disaster relief endeavor, mounting a multi-dimensional and large-scale relief operation that involves the whole of government and society, and is quickly and effectively executed. This is another vivid example of the âironcladâ friendship between China and Pakistan. China will continue to provide support to Pakistan and deepen cooperation with the country in pandemic response, disaster early warning and monitoring, and post-disaster reconstruction, among other things.

Liu Jinsong noted that some countries have tried to deflect attention from mounting domestic problems by pointing fingers at the human rights situation of other countries. Their acts can be best described by the Urdu proverb that "Thieves steal things and then go to Mecca for hajj." They also politicize, instrumentalize and weaponize human rights issues, and gang up on developing countries. Just as another Urdu proverb has put it, "The yellow dog and the jackal are brothers." China stands ready to maintain close coordination and cooperation with Pakistan on multilateral platforms to jointly safeguard the common interests of developing countries and international fairness and justice.

Liu Jinsong said that currently, many developing countries are facing energy shortages and debt plight, caused in part by geopolitical conflicts and the pandemic. But more importantly, we must not lose sight of the deep-rooted underlying problems, that is, certain countries are bent on imposing unilateral sanctions, disrupting the normal operation of the international coal, oil and natural gas markets; they also intervene in oil and gas prices out of political motives, disturbing market mechanisms; and some developed countries are snapping up energy resources, driving up commodity prices, with developing countries short of oil and gas bearing the brunt of the impact. In addition, the US Federal Reserve's aggressive interest rate hike and balance sheet reduction have accelerated the capital outflow from emerging markets and increased the debt burden of developing countries. Pakistani Foreign Minister Bilawal recently called for measures to deal with the crises and to provide support to developing countries mired in difficulties. At the Ministerial Meeting of the Group of Friends of the Global Development Initiative (GDI) recently held in New York, all parties agreed to focus on the pressing challenges and jointly address outstanding issues such as food security, poverty reduction, energy security, and disruption of industrial and supply chains. China is ready to deepen cooperation with Pakistan to jointly safeguard food and energy security and financial stability, and push for an international economic order that is more just and equitable.

Haque noted that he attended the disaster relief donation ceremony hosted by the Chinese People's Association for Friendship with Foreign Countries days ago, and was deeply touched to witness the generous donations of 125 million RMB yuan, in kind and cash, by representatives from various provinces and cities of China. He said that Pakistan will make optimum use of the aid funds and supplies, and expressed the hope that China will continue to support Pakistan in such areas as post-disaster recovery, infrastructure building, economic relief, agricultural restoration and energy supply. He underscored that Pakistan has always supported China in safeguarding its core interests, and delivered the joint statement at the UN Human Rights Council on behalf of nearly 70 countries, rejecting interference in China's internal affairs under the pretext of human rights. Pakistan is ready to work with China to implement the GDI, jointly address challenges such as energy and food security, and achieve economic recovery and sustainable development.
